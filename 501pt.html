UNDERUTILIZED SPACE CONSULTATION


OVERVIEW

Client

A software consulting-based company that utilizes the intersection of strategy, marketing, technology, and innovation to “build the software today for inventions of tomorrow.”

Client Problem

The client's award-winning open-office workspace has challenges with scaling, space usage, unrealized visions, and an underutilization of telepresence technology.

Additionally, as a consulting-based company, there is a constant flux of people in the office, ranging from 20-60 people. The client emphasizes the importance to use their space as they have intentionally designed while staying on brand.

Our Goals and Approach

We aimed to discover underlying source(s) of painpoints and offer recommendations.

We conducted interviews and observed the office's flow over the course of the consult in order to identify problems in maximizing space usage that our client faces due to the nature of a consulting.

PROCESS

INSIGHTS

Affinity Wall

Interviewed 7 stakeholders with roles ranging from management to software development and created about 300 affinity notes from interviews and observations.

FINDINGS

In addition to concerns about the makerspace, our consulting team discovered that many of the concerns and issues raised explicitly by employees and unearthed by multiple interviews revolved around restricted communication lines between employees and management.

Findings Related to Restricted Communication Flow

Our consulting team identified some areas of concern regarding communication between employees early in the project. From the initial meeting through the interviews, each employee we talked to was excited to talk about the culture of the company, which is hugely important to its success. Although the organizational structure is ostensibly flat, we found evidence in our interviews that employees have stratified themselves to a point that specific bottlenecks have formed.

Although the makerspace was the initial focus of the project, the consulting team found that several of the roadblocks to moving on the foundation of a makerspace, as well as issues with other concerns our client raised at the outset of the project, could probably be resolved by tackling the communication restrictions.

Findings Related to Makerspace

The employees who participated in the interviews for this project are excited about the makerspace idea, and many had unique ideas of what the makerspace could look like. The consulting team felt it was critical to evaluate what employees hoped the space will look like and what they hope to do with it. In the two years since the idea of founding a makerspace in The Forge originated, there are a few factors which have consistently kept Pillar from realizing the makerspace. The consulting team feels it is important that Pillar identify and address these long-standing issues before forging ahead with the makerspace.

# Findings
1 Employees have valuable ideas and relevant concerns that have gone unheard by upper management.
2 Employees have stratified themselves despite the flat organizational structure preferred by Pillar.
3 The open office concept of The Forge makes it difficult to realize the full potential of the space.
4 No one has been designated to take charge of the makerspace project.
5 The company may be moving to a new office space within a year.
6 Employees prefer time specifically dedicated to using the makerspace.
7 Employees interested in the makerspace have different ideas about who the space should serve.
8 Employees expect the makerspace to be a multipurpose space capable of accommodating varied situational needs.
9 Because of space constraints, there may be more practical uses for underutilized spaces in The Forge.

RECOMMENDATIONS

# Recommendations
1 Form rotating committees to solicit ideas from employees and spearhead projects.
2 Formalize and openly share an accurate organizational chart.
3 Add additional focus rooms & create a long-view employee calendar.
4 Appoint liaison committee in charge of makerspace.
5 Consider portability of equipment procured for the makerspace and possible transportation costs due to a move.
6 Makerspace usage can be billed to the client as part of their projects with Pillar.
7 Design the makerspace with employees in mind first.
8a If revenue is a concern, rent the space to clients or the community.
8b Encourage employees to bring their hobby projects to work or have a mechanism to gather ideas about tools and resources that can be used within the makerspace.
9 Address the space constraints before tackling the makerspace; consider a different location for the makerspace.
