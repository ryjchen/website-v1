AN INCENTIVIZING COOKING APP


OVERVIEW

Problem Framing

Often, college is the first time young adults move away from the comfort of their home to being solely responsible for everything, from succeeding in commitments to living independently. It is already draining and stressful being a student, people often neglect the importance of eating a proper meal. Many who do not cook found cooking to be too expensive, too time consuming, or that they simply do not have the skill.

Target Audience

Ann Arbor, MI. is a college town filled with predominantly students. My primary target audience is the Univeristy of Michigan students who do not have prior knowledge in cooking. My secondary target audience is the students who know the basics, but would like to find recipes that best suit their needs.

PROCESS

USER RESEARCH

Current solutions target incorrect audience, has too narrow of focus, and often face limited resrouces.

Competitive Analysis

Name: Epicurious Recipes & Food Videos

Type: app

Strengths: detailed instructions, specific ingredients list, wide range of recipes, intuitive and beautifully designed UI

Weaknesses: not beginner friendly, limited functionality (recipes and shopping list)

Name: Allrecipes Dinner Spinner

Type: app

Strengths: online community to engage users, organized collection of favorite recipes, fun "Dinner Spinner" functionality

Weaknesses: Hard to find recipes based on time and skill level, "Dinner Spinner" often results with 0 recipes, limited functionality (recipes and shopping list), user generated content results in unprofessional pictures

User Interviews

User Requirements: portability, ease of use, personalization of recipe search, and immediate gratification.

IDEATION

Design Philosophy

"A technological infrastructure that is straightforward and provides an outlet for students to learn the basics of cooking and to live a healthier life."

High-Level Overview

User follows step-by-step instructions to self-selected recipes. The more the user cooks and shares, the more points he or she earns toward redeeming coupons to grocery stores around Ann Arbor.

Solution Sketches

Brainstormed possible solutions to my design problem. The ideas were drawn from exisiting products and with the intent of having more diversity than the same solution with slightly different approaches.

Storyboarding

From the solution sketches, I sketched out 5 worksflows to convey how the solution is used to approach my design problem.

Personas

Developed personas for my target users. The intent was to diversity the characteristics amongst the personas to illustrate a wide range of user needs, motivations, skill levels, etc.

DESIGN

Paper Prototype

Used low cost materials to quickly assemble a low-fidelity mock up of my final design solution, a mobile app. The goal of this paper prototype is to illustrate user flows and interactions and be able to justify my design decisions.

Digital Prototype

Based on the feedback of user testing on the low-fidelity paper prototype, changes were made and translated into a high-fidelity digital prototype. The digital prototype has much more interactions designed and implemented. Try out the app HERE
